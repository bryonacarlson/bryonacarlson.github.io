Core Philosophy

"I take it personally when a system is down."

To me, major incident management isn't a coordination checklist or a shifting of administrative duties; it is a direct ownership function. I live by the rule that the best ability is availability. That applies to the infrastructure, but it also applies to me personally. I am fully committed to the lifecycle of an incident, ensuring that while I am on the bridge, the technical team has absolute focus.

If a situational shift handoff is required, I ensure the transition is seamless and completely documented so continuity is maintained. My personal ownership doesn't stop until service is fully restored in production.

Following service restoration, I immediately conduct the initial root cause analysis and document early indicators to ensure a clean, precise handoff to Problem Management. This baseline availability gives engineering teams the calm space they need to remediate, and it gives executive stakeholders absolute certainty that their environment is in steady hands.
